Dutch Graffiti

Graffiti came to the Netherlands in the late 70's and early 80's, inspired by graffiti writers from New York. The Dutch graffiti scene started to grow quickly in Amsterdam, with writers such as Shoe, Joker and Delta. Later it spread to the rest of Holland with an active graffiti scene in for example Rotterdam, Utrecht and Zwolle. Currently the Dutch graffiti scene is more alive than ever, influenced by social media such as Instagram where new graffiti pieces, tags and throwups from the Netherlands are posted all the time. Today there are many established Dutch writers and crews, such as Same, Defs, Fofs, Jake and Farao. Also new talent is inspired and encouraged to get foot on the ground in the Dutch graffiti scene. This site contains an impression of current graffiti in the Netherlands. All photos are shot by me. I mostly photograph graffiti on walls and along traintracks, but also occasionally on trains and subways.
